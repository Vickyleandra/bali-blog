Yummylicious!

In this section, you will find a little bit about the balinese cuisine.

Pisang Goreng

It's a dessert made with fried bananas. It can be served with syrup or honey, coconut flakes and ice cream.

Bebek betutu

A dish containing duck with different spices wrapped in palm leaves. If you want to try this traditional meal, be aware that it takes 12h to cook, so you'll probably want to give one day's notice to the restaurant."

Nasi Goreng

Fried rice, probably the most traditional dish in Indonesia in general as rice it's grown all over the country.

Sate

A sate that is not a satay. Spiced chicken or fish is mashed on a stick and barbecued.

Nasi Campur

It's a mix of different flavours, but basically contains rice, vegetables and a source of protein like fish or meat.

Mie Goreng

A dish made with fried noodles, chicken/pork/shrimp, with a side of prawn crackers, sate skewers or an egg.

Vegan meals

Vegan options are widely available in Bali.

Sambal Matah

A sambal is made of shallots, lemongrass, spices, and shrimp paste.

Nasi Tepeng

Mushy rice combined with spices and herbs, beans, chicken, eggplant and jackfruit

Mini rijstafel

A platter containing the best of the Indonesian food in small portions: rice, sates, Bebek betutu and many other delicacies.

Gado-gado

A vegetarian option is gado-gado, which is a cold salad with spinach, eggs, potato, bean sprouts, tofu and much more covered in a peanut sauce.
