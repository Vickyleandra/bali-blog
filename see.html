See the wonder of the island of the Gods

Ganesh Statues

These statues of a human bodied, elephant headed God can be seen throughout the island and is a symbol of wisdom

Waterfalls

Bali waterfalls come in a wonderful variety. From easy nature treks to hidden falls that are fit for adventurous travellers, the Waterfalls

Goa Gajah

Also known as the Elephant Cave, this cave was built to be a sanctuary and is believed to have been built as place for spiritual meditation, however, folklorists believe that it was created by the fingernail of the legendary giant Kebo Iwa.

Pura Ulun Danu Batur

This sacred Hindu temple is situated 900 meters above sea level and is locally known as Batur Temple. The temple faces west with amazing the background of Mount Batur and the lovely view of Lake Batur

Fields and forests of Ubud

Ubud is especially popular among tourists because of the location it is situated in. Lying between mountain cliffs, these fields and forests are a breathtaking sight

Beji Temple in Singaraja

This temple is dedicated to the rice goddess Dewi Sri, and is revered especially by the farmers around the area.

Kelingking Secret Point Beach

The cliffs at Kelingking Secret Point Beach form in the shape of a Tyrannosaurus Rex. It is the comparison that has caused this location to become so popular with tourists.

Candi bentar

Candi bentar, or split gateway, is a classic Balinese gateway entrance commonly found at the entrance of religious compounds, kraton palaces, or cemeteries.

Tirta Empul Temple

This temple, dedicated to the Hindu God Vishnu was founded around a large water spring around 962 A.D and is famous for its holy spring water. It is used as a source for ritual bathing.

Underwater Sculptures

Jason deCaires Taylor, a well-known underwater sculptor has created a man-made reef that can be viewed while diving between Bali and Lombok. The piece that consists of 48 life-sized figures gathered in a circle is expected to become covered in coral. making it a prime destinitation for divers.

Uluwatu Temple

This Hindu sea temple is one of six key temples believed to be Bali's spiritual pillars and is perfect for witnessing a magnificent sunset.

Bali Handara Gate

This beautiful gate is actually the entrance to a golf resort. The traditional Hindu gate symbolises the entrance from the outer world to the temple, or in this case, golf course, and plays an important role in Indonesian culture, particularly in Bali.

Rice Fields

Be enchanted by the intense green of the wonderful rice fields of Bali.

Nusa Penida

Nusa means “island” and Penida means “priests” was once believed by locals to be inhabited by dark spirits, banished to the island by the priests of Bali. However, even with such a negative belief surrounding the island, it remains a religious destination. This is because the Balinese believe in balance in the universe, in which both good and evil are necessary.
